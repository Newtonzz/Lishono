# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Lishono exists to record and teach spoken Suryoyo for everyone, across every church affiliation,
dialect region, and political view in the diaspora and beyond. We pledge to make participation a
harassment-free experience for all.

## The language is neutral ground

The Suryoyo community carries real divisions — church divisions, dialect rivalries, geopolitics.
Lishono is not a venue for any of them. Here, every family's way of speaking is data worth
keeping. Specifically, the following have no place in this project:

- **Dialect chauvinism** — claiming one dialect or village is the "real" or "pure" Suryoyo.
- **Sectarian gatekeeping** — using church affiliation to decide whose contributions count.
- **Orthography policing** — telling contributors their spelling is "wrong" (see `ORTHOGRAPHY.md`).
- **Geopolitical fights** — importing outside conflicts into entries, comments, or reviews.

## Expected behavior

- Assume good faith. A surprising spelling or pronunciation is usually a real variant, not an error.
- Preserve context rather than erase it. Add a note or variant instead of overwriting.
- Disagree about the language productively; record the disagreement as data.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, slurs, personal attacks, doxxing, trolling, and deliberate disruption. Verifiers and
admins may remove content, revoke roles, and ban accounts for violations.

## Enforcement

Report issues to the maintainers via a private GitHub issue or the contact listed in the repo.
Verification authority rests with a **council**, not a single person, precisely so that
enforcement is not captured by one faction.
